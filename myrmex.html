Myrmex
Rules • Credits • Settings

An ant colony is a place of feverish activity, where workers endlessly toil for the greater good of the superorganism. You are a worker ant, constantly moving detritus and foodstuffs from chamber to chamber. The problem is that other workers are bringing in more goods faster than you can sort out the old stuff! At the end of the day, the best that you can hope for is a chance to do it all over again tomorrow.

How to Play

Cards are ranked Ace, 2. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, (Pawn, (Court,)) Crown. (Pawns and Courts are optional and don't appear in the base game.) You can move any single top card onto a card of the next higher rank or into an empty tableau space. You can also move any set of cards in descending rank order onto a card of the next higher rank or into an empty tableau space, provided that the cards you are moving all share a common suit. If you uncover a face-down card in a tableau stack, it will be revealed. (Some game levels have no face-down cards.)

At any time you may deal eight more cards (four on the last deal) to the tops of the eight tableau piles. When you have assembled a descending sequence from Crown to Ace which share a single suit, you have completed one-sixth of your sorting task! The sequence is moved to one of the six chambers at the top of the play area.

Winning

Filling no chambers is a total loss, filling 1 to 5 chambers is a partial win, and filling all six chambers is a true victory. An easy victory takes 15 minutes or less, a sweaty victory takes 15 to 25 minutes, and more than 25 minutes is a brain buster.

You can switch to an easier or some harder variants in the Settings (click the gear icon). The rules are also available at the Decktet Wiki.

Settings

Speed: 

The card animation is helpful, but can be slow on mobile. Set the speed to 0 to turn off animation, or increase it to slow the cards down.

Enlarge

Normal card size shows the full card, but it can be hard to make out the suits this way. Press the Enlarge button at any time to zoom in to just the upper left corners of the cards, or the Normal button to unzoom.

Black Moons

For added legibility, a black sky background has been added to the moon suit. Uncheck this option to use the unadulterated artwork in your next game.
